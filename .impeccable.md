## Design Context

### Users
Ghanaian consumers shopping for smartphones, accessories, and repair parts, plus customers booking doorstep phone repairs. Mobile-heavy traffic, price-sensitive but expecting a legitimate, trustworthy storefront — not a generic template. Also technicians/admin staff (later phases) managing orders and repairs.

### Brand Personality
Precise, trustworthy, unfussy. ScrinHouse should feel like a serious, established retailer — closer to an Apple Store display than a market stall — without being cold or corporate. Confidence through restraint, not decoration.

### Aesthetic Direction
White, minimalist, monochrome (black / white / gray only — no accent color; explicit user preference, confirmed 2026-07-15). Theme respects system preference (light/dark toggle stays available, but light is the primary, carefully designed mode and dark must stay equally monochrome/restrained, not just an inverted default). Explicit anti-references: gradient hero backgrounds, pill/badge label above headlines, three-icon feature-strip grids, generic rounded-2xl cards with drop shadows, colored status badges as decoration — all called out directly by the user as "AI-looking" and rejected. References: Apple, Stripe, Nothing, Vercel, Linear (from the original brief) — restrained typography-led layouts, generous whitespace, real product photography doing the visual work instead of decorative UI chrome.

### Design Principles
1. Monochrome only — black/white/gray for all UI chrome, layout, and text. No colored accents, no colored badges/pills as decoration. Reserve color (a single desaturated red) only for genuinely destructive/error states, never for emphasis or branding.
2. No decorative containers — avoid wrapping content in bordered/shadowed cards by default; use whitespace, type scale, and alignment for hierarchy instead. Product cards are the one legitimate exception (grid of real photography needs a boundary), but keep them flat (hairline border, no shadow, no rounded-shadow combo).
3. Typography carries the hierarchy — big, confident display type (Cabinet Grotesk) for headings, a clean grotesque (Switzer) for body/UI text. Avoid icon-above-heading patterns and pill-shaped labels.
4. Real content over decoration — hero sections lead with actual product photography or a strong editorial statement, not gradients or abstract shapes.
5. Both themes are first-class — dark mode (system-driven) must be designed with the same monochrome restraint as light, not treated as an inverted afterthought.
